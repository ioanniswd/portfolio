## Market Overview Chapter Location

The financial market data ("Επισκόπηση Αγοράς Τυχερών Παιγνίων") is in:
- **2012**: Scattered in Chapter 2, pages 14–43. Limited financial breakdown; mostly activity report.
- **2013**: Chapter 2, pages 16–35. Comprehensive table covering 2009–2013.
- **2014–2016**: Chapter 2, section 2.2, starting pages ~26–45.
- **2017–2023**: Chapter 2, starting pages ~15–32.
- **2024**: Chapter 3 (restructured), pages 21–30. **GGR only — no TGR reported.**

## Data Notes

### Coverage and Methodology

- **TGR (Κύκλος Εργασιών)** = Total Gaming Revenue = total wagers staked (turnover).
- **GGR (Ακαθάριστα / Μικτά Έσοδα)** = Gross Gaming Revenue = TGR minus player payouts.
- All figures in euros (millions). Greek number format (periods as thousands separators, commas as decimals) converted to standard decimal notation.

### Year-by-Year Gaps and Caveats

**2012**
- The 2012 report is primarily an activity report (Απολογισμός) with limited financial tables.
- Financial data for 2012 is sourced from a retrospective table in the 2013 annual report.
- No separate online (internet) gambling data — the online market operated informally under Art. 50 of Law 4002/2011 and was not yet formally reported by category.

**2013**
- Online gambling figures (TGR ~1,648M, GGR ~96M) are approximate, derived from text references in the 2013 report. The regulated online market was nascent.

**2014**
- Ελληνικά Λαχεία A.E. (State Lotteries subsidiary of OPAP Group) launched 1 May 2014, so the 2014 State Lotteries figures represent only ~8 months of operation.
- Online figures are approximate, derived from report text.

**2015–2016**
- Online turnover grew substantially as more companies operated under the transitional Art. 50 regime.
- VLT machines were not yet introduced (launched late 2017).

**2017**
- VLT (Video Lottery Terminals) were introduced in the market around end of 2017, contributing a small amount to 2017 OPAP figures and growing significantly in subsequent years.
- Online total and GGR figures for 2017 are derived estimates from reported growth rates.

**2018–2019**
- Online gambling TGR and GGR for 2018 and 2019 are derived from growth rates published in the 2020 report (online TGR grew 35.85% in 2020 vs 2019; online GGR grew 34.46%).
- Casino TGR figures for 2018–2019 are calculated from bar chart values (Tables + Slots components) as no exact summary table was available for those years in isolation.

**2020**
- Severely impacted by COVID-19 pandemic restrictions (casino and OPAP shop closures, horse racing suspended for periods). Land-based categories show sharp declines.
- Online gambling surged as players migrated from land-based venues: online share of total TGR reached 72.11% (vs 53.31% in 2019).
- Πρωτοχρονιάτικο (New Year lottery) was not conducted in 2020 for the first time since the lottery's founding.
- Total market GGR fell from ~2,225M (2019 est.) to 1,621M despite online growth.

**2021**
- COVID restrictions continued through part of 2021, still affecting land-based venues.
- In August 2021, ΕΕΕΠ issued formal operating licences to 15 online gambling operators, ending the transitional Art. 50 regime. This means the 2021 online figures include ~7 months under the old regime and ~5 months under the new formal licensing.
- The 2021 report notes that online TGR and GGR exclude approximately €61M TGR and €200K GGR from three companies that operated under transitional provisions but were not granted final licences.
- The 2022 report's 2021 comparison figures (22,487.64M) differ marginally from the 2021 report's own total (22,496.02M) due to reclassification of some unlicensed operators.

**2022**
- First full year of regulated online gambling under the new licensing framework.
- Online gambling dominates TGR at 72.95% of total market (31.51% of total GGR — lower share because online has very high payout ratios ~95.59%).
- Casino TGR recovered strongly post-COVID (+78.17% vs 2021).

**2023**
- Market chapter renumbered to Chapter 3 (from Chapter 2 in earlier years).
- Online gambling reached 74.91% of total TGR and 33.46% of total GGR.
- ODIE horse racing recovered to near 2019 levels.
- Total market GGR reached 2,592M, up 10.49% vs 2022.

**2024**
- The 2024 report is structured differently: **it reports GGR only** for the market overview table — no TGR figures are provided at the market summary level. This is a significant structural change from all prior years.
- Ιπποδρομίες A.E. (horse racing operator) suspended operations from 30 January 2024, as the registered active horse population fell below the 300-horse threshold required under the concession agreement.
- Only 8 casino businesses operated (vs 10 in prior years); Καζίνο Ρίο licence revoked, Καζίνο Αιγαίου (Σύρου) suspended for renovation.
- Total market GGR reached 2,879M, up 11.0% vs 2023.
- Online GGR grew 23.0% to 1,067M, reaching 37.1% of total GGR.
- OPAP land-based GGR grew 5.2% to 1,436M, representing 49.9% of total GGR.

### Online Gambling Methodology Note

Online figures for 2012–2021 cover companies operating under the transitional regime of Article 50, Law 4002/2011. These companies declared revenues to the tax authority but were not formally licensed by ΕΕΕΠ. Starting August 2021, the market transitioned to a formal licensing regime under ΕΕΕΠ supervision, enabling much more detailed and audited reporting (as seen in the detailed 2021–2024 online breakdowns by game category).

### OPAP Category Note

"OPAP" (Παίγνια ΟΠΑΠ Α.Ε., Επίγειο) covers all land-based games operated through OPAP retail shops: PAME STOIXHMA (sports betting), KINO (numbers game), VLT (Video Lottery Terminals, introduced late 2017), TZOKER (jackpot lottery), LOTTO, SUPER 3, PROTO, EXTRA 5, PROPO, and PROPOGOAL. Online OPAP games (OPAP also holds an online licence) are counted within the "Online" / "Διαδίκτυο" category from 2021 onward.

### State Lotteries Note

"State_Lotteries" (Ελληνικά Λαχεία / Κρατικά Λαχεία) covers: SKRATS (scratch cards — largest revenue), Λαϊκό Λαχείο, Εθνικό Λαχείο, and the seasonal Πρωτοχρονιάτικο (New Year lottery, not conducted in 2020).

### ODIE / Horse Racing Note

ODIE (Οργανισμός Διεξαγωγής Ιπποδρομιών Ελλάδος) operated horse racing betting until 2015. From April 2015, the concession was transferred to Ιπποδρομίες A.E. (an OPAP Group subsidiary). The category is labelled "ODIE" throughout for consistency. Operations were suspended from 30 January 2024.
